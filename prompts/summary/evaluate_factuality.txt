1 = (Complely unfaithful): The summary is largely inaccurate, containing frequent errors, distortions, or fabrications that render it unreliable.
2 = (Mostly unfaithful): The output misses key facts or introduces incorrect information, with multiple errors that change the intended meaning in important ways.
3 = (Partially faithful): The main points are present, yet several details are slightly altered, omitted, or over‑generalised, leading to a noticeable but not critical loss of fidelity.

5 = (Completely faithful): The summary reproduces the source information exactly, with no distortion, omission, or addition; every claim is 100 % accurate.

2. Completeness
1 = (Incomplete): The output fails to cover the core content, leaving most of the important information omitted.
2 = (Partial): Captures the general topic but omits key sections or facts, resulting in a significant loss of the source’s meaning.
3 = (Adequate): Several notable pieces of information are missing, but the remaining content still conveys a reasonable overview.
4 = (Strong): The majority of essential content is present, with only one or two minor omissions that do not hinder understanding.
5 = (Comprehensive): All relevant information from the source is included; no important detail is left out.
